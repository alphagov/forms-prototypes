GOV.UK Forms is a new platform that makes it easy to create accessible online forms for the GOV.UK website.

Because it’s still in the early stages of development, the features available are limited. So it will not be suitable for creating all types of online form.

Create an account to try it out

If you’re from a central government organisation you can create an account to try GOV.UK Forms out and start making forms.

Some departments are restricting who can create an account from their organisation. We’ll tell you if this affects you.

You’ll need to use your government email address to create an account.

{{ govukButton({ text: 'Create an account', href: "sign-up", isStartButton: true }) }}

Who can make forms live

When you first create an account, you’ll be able to create and preview forms but you will not be able to make forms live.

You’ll only be able to make forms live if:

  • you’re from a central government organisation that publishes content on the GOV.UK website
  • someone from your organisation has agreed to a ‘Memorandum of Understanding’ with GOV.UK Forms
  • you’ve been given permission to make forms live from your organisation’s GOV.UK Forms admin

You’ll be able to get help with this process from your organisation’s GOV.UK Forms admin or from the GOV.UK Forms team.

Subscribe to updates about GOV.UK Forms

If GOV.UK Forms doesn’t meet your needs yet, you can subscribe to our mailing list to get updates about new features.
